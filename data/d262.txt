London markets jumped this morning, driven by industrial stocks climbing on an ongoing rally in commodity prices.

The capital’s premier FTSE 100 index added 0.78 per cent to reach 7,522.48 points, while the domestically-focused FTSE 250 index, which is more aligned to the health of the UK economy, soared 1.53 per cent to 22,262.36 points.

Miners led the FTSE 100 higher this morning as commodities continued their upward trend since the start of the year.

Nickel hit a 10-year high recently, while oil prices rose above $90 a barrel for the first time in a decade.

Polymetal and Fesnillo were among the best performers on the capital’s top index, adding 1.84 per cent and 1.82 per cent respectively.

Susannah Streeter, senior investment and market analyst at Hargreaves Lansdown, said: “The FTSE 100 has the feel good factor on the first day of February with the index rising by 0.7 per cent in early trade helped by tech stocks clawing back some losses.”

“There seems to be renewed enthusiasm for some of the stocks investors had taken flight from in fright about the impact of higher interest rates on the value of future earnings.”

Meanwhile, on the FTSE 250, travel stocks were the bright spot.

Train ticket booking app Trainline and bus operator National Express threatened the summit of the biggest risers table, climbing 3.57 per cent and four per cent respectively.

The pound gained ground against the dollar, strengthening 0.38 per cent to buy $1.3496.
