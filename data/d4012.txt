A MAN and teenager have been arrested after a boy was taken to hospital with serious injuries after he was attacked with a pool cue in Southampton.

Police are investigating an assault with a pool cue on Sunday, January 30, in Millbrook.

At around 8.45pm, officers on patrol attended an incident at a bus stop in Windermere Avenue, Millbrook, where a 14-year-old boy was being assaulted by a man and two boys.

The victim sustained serious injuries to his left leg and was taken to hospital.

A 14-year-old boy from Southampton was arrested on suspicion of grievous bodily harm with intent and was bailed with conditions until February 28 while enquiries continue.

A 33-year-old man from Southampton was arrested on suspicion of grievous bodily harm with intent and possession of an offensive weapon in a public place and was bailed with conditions until February 27 while enquiries continue.

A spokesperson from Hampshire Constabulary said: "Enquiries are ongoing to locate those involved in the incident.

"Did you see what happened? Do you have dash cam, CCTV or ring doorbell footage of the moments before the incident?

"Please contact us on 101 or report online, quoting 44220041208."
