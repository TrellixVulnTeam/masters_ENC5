10. And finally, six more weeks of winter — maybe.

Punxsutawney Phil emerged from his tree stump in Pennsylvania this morning and saw his shadow, predicting a longer winter. His counterpart in New York, Staten Island Chuck, signaled warmer weather ahead. No matter which tea leaves you prefer to read, Groundhog Day remains the one day in the U.S. that is earmarked for an animal.

Beyond their troublesome garden reputation, not much is known about Marmota monax. But a study of groundhogs in Maine, now in its third decade, is shedding light on their social habits: These groundhogs aren’t solitary, as many assume. They form a loose-knit clan with subtle, kinship-based relationships. Researchers hope the work may help people understand the benefits of complex social networks.
