This is the moment a mass brawl erupted in Glasgow's east end ahead of the Celtic v Rangers clash at Parkhead Stadium.

Horrifying footage shows droves of revellers fighting in Duke Street not long before 7pm on Wednesday.

Carnage broke out in the clip where punters can be seen pouring out of pub, The Alexandra and spilling down to The Crown Creighton.

Screaming, shouting and sirens can be heard as droves of police officers scramble out of vans and pull thugs apart in the street.

Motors are forced to stop on the main road which runs right through the east end of the city.

Smashed glass is also heard in the shameful clip shared with the Daily Record by one resident.

Some locals watched on in horror as huge crowds caused a mass disturbance in the area as officers desperately try to move them on.

A massive stream of revellers are then spotted sprinting up Cumbernauld Road, which runs past Duke Street train station.

It comes as Celtic take on Rangers at nearby Parkhead Stadium.

The Scottish Premiership clash kicked off at 7.45pm.

No allegations of injuries or arrests have been made at this stage.

The exact circumstances surrounding the incident are unknown at this time.

Police Scotland have been contacted for comment.
