BEAUMONT, Texas — (Federal Bureau of Prisons) – On Monday, January 31, at approximately 11:30 a.m., multiple inmates were observed fighting at the United States Penitentiary (USP) Beaumont in Beaumont.

Responding staff immediately secured the area. Inmate Guillermo Riojas and inmate Andrew Pineda were transported to a local hospital for life-threatening injuries and they died. Two additional inmates were transported to a local hospital for further medical assessment and treatment.

The Federal Bureau of Investigation was notified. No staff or other inmates were injured and at no time was the public in danger.

Mr. Riojas was a 54-year-old male who was sentenced in the Eastern District of California to a 456-month for Carjacking and Interference With Interstate Commerce. He had been in custody at USP Beaumont since May 1, 2013.

Mr. Pineda was a 34-year-old male who was sentenced in the Central District of California to a 78-month for Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Conspiracy. He had been in custody at USP Beaumont since February 16, 2021.

USP Beaumont is a high security facility that currently houses 1,372 male offenders.
