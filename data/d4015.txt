A GROUP of men have been arrested in connection with Class A drugs supply in Southampton.

Police were called to Briarswood, Shirley Warren, by neighbours reporting a disturbance at a property there on Sunday (January 30).

On attendance, officers discovered a large quantity of cash and evidence of drug dealing at the scene. A 27-year-old man from Southampton was arrested on suspicion of being concerned in the supply of a class A drug, racially or religiously aggravated intentional harassment, and two counts of assault by beating of an emergency worker. The second, a 35-year-old man from the city, was arrested on suspicion of being concerned in the supply of a class A drug, and two counts of racially or religiously aggravated intentional harassment. A 36-year-old man, also from Southampton, was arrested on suspicion of being concerned in the supply of a class A drug, alongside a 23-year-old man for the same reason. All four were released under investigation while enquiries continue.
