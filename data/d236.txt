5. Gov. Cuomo emerged as one of the faces of the pandemic with his daily updates after COVID-19 slammed the city of New Rochelle in March, criticizing the federal response to the virus and becoming a regular on the nightly newscasts (including a CNN interview with his brother Chris). But the state also suffered 950,000 positive tests and 37,000 deaths, and Cuomo — who wrote a book on the crisis — was criticized for the deaths of 6,500 people in state nursing homes.
